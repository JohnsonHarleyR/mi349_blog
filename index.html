How Cool is JavaScript?

October 9, 2024

Harley J

Some may find this odd, but I love working in JavaScript. It is such a versatile language! It has all the power of a back-end language, yet one can directly use it to manipulate the UI. You can make things spin around the page and create all kinds of animations. You can make things happen directly on the page without having to refresh the page. You can create games! Oh, can you create games!

I will admit it, when I first began coding in JavaScript, I found it difficult and frustrating compared to other languages. It is called the "wild west" of languages for a reason. There are so many unexpected and difficult errors that can happen. The functions and variables do not require types, so the error messages give you little information about what goes wrong at times. When first learning, I would add so many console log messages just to figure out what was going on, because often, I didn't even know where the errors were occurring in the code. It was a struggle to learn, yet rewarding in the end.

Once I finally understood most of the ins and outs of JavaScript, however, it transformed into the most freeing, fun language I have ever written in. I soon went on to create all sorts of games with it. I realized there is just so much you can do! Trust me, it is a language worth learning, you just have to stick it out until you reach a level where you can do whatever you want with it!

Color Me Shocked, This Girl Likes to Code!

October 3, 2024

Harley J

Hello, my name is Harley and I am one of the rare women who enjoys coding. This is my first blog post, so I figured I should tell you a little about my background.

I have been fascinated by computers since age 4 when my grandpa started teaching me to use his brand new Windows 95 desktop computer and teaching me how to type. I learned to do this through elementary school as well. When I was about 11 years old, my grandpa bought a new desktop and we got the internet for the first time. Living with my grandparents and being an only child, I spent a lot of time on the internet, especially on a website called NeoPets.com. It was here that I first started to learn how to code web pages for my virtual pets. NeoPets offered information on how kids can start learning to code, so I did just that. I found it so much fun and so fascinating to be able to create my own web page! Back then, we didn't use a lot of CSS. HTML actually had a syntax to change text color, size, background color, etc. right inside of the tags. (I realize you can still do this with the "style" tag, but you can no longer do it the way you once could.)

Throughout middle school and high school, I continued learning to code, both on my own and through classes. I took a web design course where the teacher believed I was cheating because she thought my web page was too good, but in truth, I already knew how to do most of what was taught in the class. I also learned Adobe Flash and some of what was called "ActionScript". I learned and became proficient in Adobe PhotoShop as well, and in making games with a program called Game Maker. I learned a language called "Visual Basic" in another class, which first introduced me to back-end programming. My senior year of high school, I took a college-level, year-long course about programming and the language of Python. I was the only girl in the class, but I excelled. I finished the lessons so quickly that I went on to create my own games and programs and began tutoring the other students in the class. At the end of the year, the teacher gave me an award for "Most Outstanding Programmer".

That was all the way back in 2009. Through most of my 20s, I mostly had fun, to be honest. I was focused on creating art and with having my laptop stolen, I stopped programming for quite a while. Toward the end of my 20s though, after failing to make a living as an artist, I learned that developers make good money and remembered that I once had a passion for this. That was when I completed a JAVA coding boot camp and obtained my first job as a developer. I worked with back-end languages like C# and SQL, but also taught myself JavaScript on the side. I realized that I greatly enjoy languages like this, so I transitioned to working more in the UI as a React developer. I enjoyed this sweet spot between the back-end and front-end where I can utilize both my creativity and problem-solving skills.

In the end, I wanted to move back home, but struggled to find another developer job here with only a boot camp certificate and associate's degree, so I am now completing my bachelor's at MSU. I cannot wait to get back to being a developer!
